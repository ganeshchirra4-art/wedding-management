Go Green on Your Big Day

The Ultimate Guide to planning a Sustainable Development

A wedding is one of the most beautiful celebrations of love and culture,
but it often comes with an environmental cost. From excessive food waste to single-use décor, traditional weddings can leave a significant carbon footprint.
However, with mindful planning,
you can have a sustainable wedding that respects cultural wedding traditions while minimizing environmental impact.

Sustainable Wedding Venues

Choosing the right venue plays a crucial role in minimizing your wedding’s carbon footprint.
Open-air spaces, heritage sites, or eco-resorts powered by renewable energy are great options.
South Indian weddings held in temple courtyards benefit from natural lighting, ventilation, and the cool touch of granite,
minimizing electricity use. Meanwhile, North Indian palace weddings can embrace sustainability by incorporating energy-efficient lighting and
supporting local vendors during the busy baraat season!

Ethical & Local Wedding Attire

Instead of synthetic or heavily embellished fabrics, opt for handwoven textiles that support local artisans.
South Indian brides can wear traditional Kanjivaram or Pochampally sarees,
while North Indian brides can choose Banarasi or Chanderi weaves. Grooms can choose khadi or organic cotton sherwanis for a more eco-friendly option.
Reusing heirloom outfits or renting designer wear also helps reduce fashion waste.

Minimal-Waste Wedding Decor

Decor plays a huge role in setting the mood of a wedding, but it often leads to waste. Instead of single-use plastic décor,
use locally sourced flowers, potted plants, and fabric drapes. North Indian weddings can feature vibrant marigolds and mogra garlands,
while South Indian weddings can incorporate banana leaves, betel nuts, and brass lamps for a traditional yet eco-friendly aesthetic.
Renting décor instead of buying also reduces waste. .
